Tourist Attractions

Universal Studios Singapore is Southeast Asia's first and only Universal Studios theme park. It features 24 rides and attractions, including a pair of dueling coasters that brush past one another in several near misses in their aerial combat.With 18 attractions created or redesigned for Singapore, Universal Studios Singapore promises an experience that you will not forget.

Sentosa is Singapore's premier island resort getaway. This 500-hectare resort island is home to an exciting array of themed attractions, award-winning spa retreats, and resort accommodations alongside lush rainforests and golden sandy beaches. Popular attractions among business and leisure visitors include Wings of Time - an award winning night show, the iconic Sentosa Merlion, as well as Madame Tussauds Singapore.

Gardens by the Bay is a huge, colourful, futuristic park in the bay area of Singapore. Among the standout features are the famous Supertree structures. These offer an impressive skywalk over the gardens, with oversized seashell-shaped greenhouses that recreate chilly mountain climates.There are hundreds of trees and plants to discover within, making Gardens by the Bay a great destination for both kids and grownups, and simply for those looking for a respite with stunning views.

Shopping Destinations

Marina Bay Sands is a luxury hotel and integrated resort that also offers a high-end shopping experience at The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands. The Shoppes is home to numerous designer and luxury brands, from Louis Vuitton to Chanel, alongside fine dining restaurants and cafes. The architecture itself is remarkable, with a beautiful glass facade and an indoor canal where visitors can take boat rides. This mall also hosts a casino, the ArtScience Museum, and access to the famous rooftop infinity pool, making it more than just a shopping destination.

VivoCity is one of Singapore's largest and most popular malls, known for its variety of stores that cater to all ages and tastes. Located along the waterfront in the HarbourFront area, it's the gateway to Sentosa Island. VivoCity boasts a mix of international and local brands, a large cinema complex, and plenty of dining options. One of its highlights is the rooftop Sky Park, which has open spaces for relaxation and beautiful views of Sentosa. It's a family-friendly spot with everything from fashion to tech stores, as well as play areas for kids.

Bugis Street Market is a vibrant, bustling market known for affordable shopping, streetwear, and souvenirs. It’s one of the best spots in Singapore to experience local culture and find bargains, with hundreds of small stalls selling everything from clothes, accessories, and electronics to food and drinks. The market is famous for its inexpensive fashion finds and is particularly popular among young locals and tourists alike. Its lively atmosphere, with narrow alleys and colorful storefronts, makes it an exciting place to explore.
